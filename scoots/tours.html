Brief History of Cozumel

The history of Cozumel is rich and best experienced at your own pace. Mayans first settled the island nearly two thousand years ago, and the marks they left on the island still stand to this day, begging to be explored.

San Gervasio, the largest of the ruins on Cozumel, had a humble temple dedicated to the worship of Ixchel, their goddess of fertility. Ancient Mayans would journey to the island to pay tribute, hoping to enrich their lives and live our their dreams. In fact, Cozumel was so important to the ancient Mayan civilization that historians consider Cozumel to be as important to the ancient Mayans as Rome is to the history and culture of Europe.

In your adventure to Cozumel, you may come across sites like a cenote, a well that was also symbolic of a portal to a realm called “The Netherworld”, or Xibalba as they called it.
